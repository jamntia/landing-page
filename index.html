Projects

Catagram

I wanted to start my summer off with a big project, and many people recommended that I clone a popular social media website. I went with Instagram, and despite countless road bumps, bugs, and other technical issues, I successfully made a full-stack web application that functions similarly to instagram.com. I used React.js for my front end and Google Firebase as my BaaS.

Although this project was fun to do, to me the idea itself was kind of boring. I thought to myself, “Instagram already exists, so in terms of functionality, what good is a clone of it?” This is where I decided to add a little twist to my project. What if I coded Instagram, but made it so that you could only upload a photo if there is a cat somewhere in the image?

I spent some time learning about convolutional neural networks and how I could use them for object detection. I didn't want my neural network to be a “black box,” where I just give it an input and get an output without really understanding what happened in between. I studied topics like backpropagation, Fast Fourier Transforms, and gradient descent to get a grasp on how networks learn. The model I ended up creating utilizes YOLOv5 (YOLO standing for a “You Only Look Once” algorithm) to detect if there's a cat in a given image with a 97.8% accuracy!

I deployed this model and connected it to my web app. If an image passes the model's test, it will post. Or else, it will tell the user that the model couldn't detect any cats in the image. The final result is something I like to call “Catagram!”

Speech.ai

Two of my friends and I formed a group and competed in Rutgers University's 24-hour hackathon "HackRU S24." We decided to compete under the track of "Best AI Application Built with CloudFlare."

Our inspiration stemmed from a common challenge: mastering public speaking. Personally, I have always struggled with my speech, enough to the point where I attended speech therapy in the past. Drawing from personal experiences and recognizing the widespread difficulty in this domain, we set out to develop a solution.

Our project records audio from the user and saves it as a .mp4 file. It then analyzes the recording using Cloudflare's APIs including Speech to Text, WorkersAI, and Sentiment Analysis. The speech is then critiqued based on the number of filler phrases used, the rate at which the user is talking, grammatical errors, and the tone that the speech gives off. Lastly, speech.ai returns a score ranging from 1-100 as well as why that score was given.

We competed amongst 300+ students at the hackathon, and ended up placing 1st in our track! Overall, it was a great learning experience, and I can't wait for my next hackathon.

Stop and Frisk

New York has a law called “Stop-and-Frisk” that allows police officers to “stop, interrogate and search New York City citizens on the sole basis of reasonable suspicion.” I was born in the Bronx, and I still have family that lives there, so this topic was close to home for me. The law has been subject to more than its fair share of controversy over the years, so I decided to analyze what was going on.

I got 4 datasets that describe every reported arrest NYPD made under the Stop and Frisk law from 2013, 2014, 2021, and 2022. I queried each dataset with Java (had I known Python or R at the time, I probably would've used one of them instead) and tried to find common trends. Luckily, my dataset included the demographics of each person who was arrested, including race, gender, weight, and age.

I got some pretty interesting results; for example, I found that people of color were arrested at a much higher rate than other demographics. I also noticed that younger people are more likely to be stopped than older people.

This project was the first time I've ever worked with analyzing a dataset, and it forced me to learn new search algorithms to optimize my code. In fact, this project is what got me interested in the field of data science, and I'm glad I did it. Not only did I learn new tricks in the realm of computer science, but I also got to apply them to a modern issue that affects people I care about.
